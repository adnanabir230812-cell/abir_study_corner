## Slide 2

What is plant pathology ?

Plant Pathology (Phytopathology): Greek Word
 
Three words: Phyton + Pathos + logos 
                          Plant + suffering/ailments + study/knowledge 

Definition: Plant Pathology is that branch of agricultural, botanical or biological science.


---

## Slide 3

What is plant pathology ? 
Plant pathology  deals- 
Study of the causes, etiology, resulting losses and management/ control of plant diseases.
Study of the organisms and environmental factors that cause diseases in plants, mechanisms by which they cause disease, and methods of managing diseases and reducing damage.


---

## Slide 4

Why do we study Plant Pathology?

To study the biotic, abiotic and environmental causes of plant diseases
To study the interaction(s) between the plant and the pathogen
To study the  mechanism(s) of disease development.
To develop management strategy of the diseases


---

## Slide 5

What is the importance of Plant Pathology in Agriculture ?
Plant pathology can and should contribute in each of these respects—
by assessing the immediate and potential dangers to crops from diseases
by forecasting their incidence and severity
by deploying the best methods of control in the short and longer term 
by evaluating the risks particularly by integrated use of the management practices.

## Slide 8

Ancient Period

Theophrastus (300-286 B.C.) 
 Study and write about the diseases (rust, mildews, blight)of trees, cereals and legumes in his book enquiry into plants.

 His approach was observational and speculative rather than experimental.

 Theory of spontaneous generation
 
 2 Books : Historia Plantarum
                  De Causis Plantarum

## Slide 11

PRE-MODERN/ RENAISSANCE PERIOD/AUTOGENIC ERA (17th –mid 19th)

1675 A. D.-Anton van Leeuwenhoek (Holland) 
Invented compound microscope in 1675
Examined anatomy of plants and body of filamentous fungus, algae, protozoa, sperm cells, blood cells and bacteria.
In 1683 he described bacteria seen with this microscope

Father of Microbiology

## Slide 15


![Diagram: Slide 15 Image 1](/uploads/1.0 Introduction and History of Plant Pathology_s15_img1.jpg)

Anton De Bary (1853 A. D).Germany) 

Established that fungi are the causes, not result of plant diseases

Renamed the casual organism of late blight as Phytophthora infestans.

He discovered heteroecious nature of rust fungi

Detailed life cycles of downy mildew

He wrote a book named “Morphology and Physiology of fungi, lichens and Myxomycetes” (1866).

Father of Modern Mycology

Louis Pasteur
Established the germ theory
In 1864, Pasteur invented pasteurization
in 1880, made the first vaccine against the chicken cholera.

Germ theory 
 Microorganisms were produced from preexisting microorganisms
Most infectious diseases were caused by germs.
