## Slide 17

Coastal Vegetation

There is

710 km coastline in the south of Bangladesh from west to east

. The shrub & tree vegetation was scanty & scattered along the coastline. Even naturally occurring lightly dense vegetation did not exist in any part of the coastal belt except for areas with the natural mangrove forests. The long shoreline was virtually barren & exposed to wind & waves. There was a need to bring the area under tree cover by artificial regeneration.

The coastal areas of Bangladesh experience cyclone damage almost every year. During the period

1960 to 1970, eight severe cyclones were recorded

.

The Forest Department of Bangladesh initiated mangrove afforestation in 1966.

Bangladesh is one of the leading countries where man made afforestation program with mangrove species is widely practiced. The

primary objective was to save lives & properties of the coastal dwellers

. The

secondary objectives

of the coastal plantation were to (i)

reclamation & stabilization of newly accreted

land

&

acceleration of further accretion

, (ii)

production of timber & fuel wood, & (iii) creation of employment opportunity in coastal areas.

Mangrove vegetation has great significance on ecological aspects and livelihood dev. In

## Slide 23

How does succession of mangrove formation in coastal region greatly

affcted

by the gradual elevation of the sea coast?

The succession of mangroves in Bangladesh's coastal regions is significantly affected by the

gradual elevation of the sea coast due to changes in salinity

,

soil composition, tidal inundation, and vegetation zonation.

Elevated areas experience

reduced tidal flooding

and

nutrient availability

,

slowing mangrove growth

and altering species composition. This

disrupts biodiversity

and ecosystem

balance

, compounded by human activities like

shrimp farming and urbanization

.

These challenges highlight the need for

conservation to sustain mangrove ecosystems vital for coastal protection

and biodiversity.

## Slide 24

How can coastal vegetation be conserved?

Strategies to Conserve Coastal Vegetation in Bangladesh:

Reforestation and Plantation:

Restore degraded areas with native species like mangroves.

Protected Areas:

Establish sanctuaries and reserves with strict enforcement of conservation laws.

Sustainable Land Use:

Promote eco-friendly practices in shrimp farming and agriculture.

Community Engagement:

Involve local communities in conservation efforts with awareness and alternative livelihoods.

Research and Innovation:

Study salt-tolerant species and use technology to monitor vegetation.

Climate Change Adaptation:

Reduce emissions, adapt to rising sea levels, and use vegetation for carbon sequestration.

Policy and Legislation:

Enforce stricter penalties for illegal activities and update policies for climate resilience.
