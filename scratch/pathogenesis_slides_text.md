## Slide 8

Symbiotic Relationship is a type of parasitism, explain.

Symbiotic relationship

refers to any close and long-term interaction between two organisms

, including mutualism, commensalism, and parasitism.

Parasitism

is a specific type of symbiosis where one organism, the

parasite

, benefits

by exploiting the host for nutrients, water, or shelter, causing harm to the host

. Pathogen like fungi (

Puccinia

graminis

) is a plant parasite. These pathogens attach to, penetrate, infect, and invade host tissues, leading to symptoms such as necrosis, wilting, and reduced yield.

While all parasitism is symbiotic, not all symbiosis is parasitic, as mutualism and commensalism do not harm the host.
