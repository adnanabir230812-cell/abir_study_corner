## Slide 1

Productivity

The term “productivity” refers to the process through which organisms (e.g. a trophic level or ecosystem) assimilate carbon.

Primary producers (autotrophs) do this through

photosynthesis

; Secondary producers (heterotrophs) do it through the

assimilation of the organic carbon in their food.

Remember that all organic carbon in the food web is

ultimately derived from primary production.

## Slide 4

Primary Production

Same as primary productivity, but usually expressed for a whole ecosystem e.g., tons year

-1

for a lake, cornfield, forest, etc.

Gross Primary Production (GPP):

Total amount of CO

2

reduced to organic carbon by the plants per unit time.

Autotrophic Respiration (RA):

Total amount of organic carbon that is respired (oxidized to CO

2

) by plants per unit time.

Net Primary Production (NPP): GPP - RA

The amount of organic carbon produced by plants that is not consumed by their own respiration.

It is the increase in plant biomass in the absence of herbivores.

---

## Slide 5

Net Community Production

For an entire ecosystem, some of the NPP of the plants is consumed (& respired) by herbivores (RH) & decomposers & oxidized back to CO

2

(RH). The amount of carbon that is left is called NCP.

Organic C produced through photosynthesis that is not lost through RA (autotrophic respiration) or RH (herbivorous respiration) is called NCP.

NPP = GPP - RA

NCP = GPP - RA - RH = NPP - RH

## Slide 7

Secondary Productivity

The rate of production of new biomass by heterotrophs is called secondary productivity.

The productivity of heterotrophs, such as animals, is called secondary productivity. Secondary production is sometimes defined to

only include the consumption of primary producers by herbivorous consumers

(with tertiary production referring to carnivorous consumers), but is more

commonly

defined to

include all biomass generation by heterotrophs.
