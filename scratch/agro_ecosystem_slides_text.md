# Course Slides: 4. Agro-ecosystem

## Slide 1

AGROECOSYSTEM

The

agroecosystem

is an ecological system modified by humans to produce agricultural products.

Ecologically such systems are less complex than natural systems, but are made complex by the imposition of

socio-economic values & defined goals.

Agroecosystems

are defined as plant & animal communities that interact with the physical & chemical environment & are modified by humans to produce food,

fibre

, fuel, & other products for human consumption & processing will be done.

## Slide 2

Properties/ Features of Agroecosystem

Productivity:

Net increment of values products per unit resources

(land, labor, energy, capital) & is commonly measured as

annual

yield/hectare.

Stability:

A degree to which productivity remains constant, in spite of normal small-scale fluctuation in environmental variables

such as climate or in the economic condition in the market.

Sustainability:

The ability of the system to maintain its productivity when subjected to stress or perturbation

.

Stress

is defined as regular, sometimes continuous, relatively small & predictable disturbance

, e.g. effect of growing soil salinity.

Perturbation

by contrast is an irregular, in frequent, relatively long & unpredictable disturbance

such as drought or a flood.

Equitability:

A measure of how evenly the produce of agroecosystem is distributed among its human beneficiaries

. The more equitable the system, the more evenly are the products to fed shared among the population of the farm, village, regions or nation.

Components of

Agroecosystem

Biotic components:

The biotic components of the system are the

crop plants, animals & the microorganisms

present in the soil.

Crops & weeds

are the

primary

producers while the

secondary producers

are the

herbivores

comprising

insects, birds & small mammals

. The decomposers are mostly species of

fungi, microorganisms & nematodes

etc.

Crop plants:

Of the numerous species of plants, only about

7000

have been tried as

agricultural

crops & only

150

species have been cultivated on a

large scale

. The

world’s food production

is provided by

only 14 crop

species -

wheat, rice, maize, potatoes, sweet potatoes, sugarcane, sugar beet, common beans, soybeans, barley, sorghum, coconut, & bananas

. Of these,

6 species

provide directly or indirectly

80% of the total calories

consumed by human beings. Overall

globally crops occupy 31% of agricultural areas

and the

remaining area

is occupied by

pasture

& this varies region wise. In South Asia,

91% of the cropland

is under

annual crops

such as

wheat, rice and soybean

, while

perennial

crops such as

coffee, tea, fruits, sugarcane

occupy the

remainder

. Though crops are maintained as pure populations,

weeds grow along

with the crops despite best efforts to control them, by

adjusting their life cycle.

## Slide 4

Components of

Agroecosystem

Consumer:

The

homogenous

plantation of crops in the fields permits

very few animals to live & thrive

in the

agroecosystem

. The crop plants provide

shelter, site for nesting, protection from enemies & predators

. Food chains in

agroecosystems

are

simple with only 2 or 3 trophic levels

. Activities like

ploughing

& tilling

the soil

disturb it

to quite an extent, often

changing

the

micro flora & fauna

completely so that entirely new conditions are created. Also the crop communities provide scant shelter to mammals & only

small mammals, birds make temporary visits.

Decomposers:

Decomposer activity is

generally less

in agro-ecosystems due to tillage in crop fields, application of

chemical fertilizers, pesticides, herbicides & lack of organic matter in the soil

. The soil is full of microorganisms such as

bacteria,

actinomycetes

, fungi, algae, viruses & protozoa

.

Bacteria and actinomycetes

are the

most numerous

of microbial groups present in the soil.

## Slide 5

Components of

Agroecosystem

Abiotic Components:

Abiotic components of the

agroecosystem

are

soil, inorganic nutrients, climate, water, atmosphere & solar radiation

.

Soil

acts as a

nutrient pool

& forms the environment of the

roots & microorganisms

. Soil in

agroecosystems

is

oversimplified

because the upper layers are

disturbed due to farming practices

& extra inputs of energy & matter by the farmers in the form of manure fertilizers, fossil fuels & water. Often

extra salts are leached due to water logging

&

irrigation patterns

. The soils are

generally poor in organic matter

content as the crops are harvested & removed to be used as food & fodder.

## Slide 8

AGROECOSYSTEM VS. NATURAL ECOSYSTEM

Natural ecosystem is closed, or at least, unmanaged ecosystem

Closed ecosystem—all elements recycled through ecosystem—not often pure closed ecosystems anymore—humans frequently involved

Agroecosystem is an open ecosystem, or managed ecosystem:

Producer moves plants, animals, environmental factors (fertilizers, feed) in & out of ecosystem

Will not continue on its own without management

If left alone, would progress toward closed ecosystem, but probably not the same as original ecosystem before agriculture without human input again
